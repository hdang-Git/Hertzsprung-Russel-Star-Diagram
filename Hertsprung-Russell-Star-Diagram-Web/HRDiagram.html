What is the Hertzsprung-Russell Diagram?

The Hertzsprung-Russell Diagram is a significant tool to aid in the understanding of stellar evolution and development of current technology we use everyday. The diagram plots stars using the properties of temperature and luminosity (luminosity is a star's brightness).


How to read the Hertzsprung-Russell Diagram

The prominent diagonal goes from hot and bright (upper left) to cooler and dimmer (lower right). This is called the main sequence. On the lower left, white drarves are found. Above the main sequence are subgiants, giants, supergiants. Plots are stars on the graph measuring the star's brightness vs. spectral classifications or effective temps.
