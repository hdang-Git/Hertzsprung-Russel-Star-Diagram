Why is astronomy important?

AT&T uses Image Reduction and Analysis Facility (IRAF) which is software written by National Optical Astronomy Observatory to analyze computer programs. The iPhone's camera is a charge-coupled device (CCD) converting movement of electrical charge to digital value. This instrument was originally developed by astronomers.


How is it beneficial to society?

Astronomy excites the imagination

We take pleasure in looking through telescopes, visiting planetariums, and exploring the unknown. Astronomy sites are the most popular science sites on the internet today. Astronomy also fuels success in business and growth of NASA.

Aerospace

GPS satellites rely on astronomical objects like quasars and distant galaxies to determine accurate positions.

Energy

Technology gained from imaging x-rays is now used to monitor fusion where two atomic nuclei combine to form a heavier nucleus which may be an answer to clean energy.

Medicine

Astronomy struggles to see increasingly faint objects; medicine struggles to see things obscured in the human body. Building space-based telescopes requires an extremely clean environment to avoid dust particles from obscuring mirrors and instruments are used in hospitals today.

Everyday Life

Airports utilize advances in astronomy. X-ray observatory technology is used in X-ray luggage belts. A gas chromatograph, an instrument designed for a Mars mission, is used to analyze luggage for explosives.

Einstein's theory of relativity

Law of physics are the same for all non-accelerating observers; speed of light in a vacuum is the same no matter the speed at which the observer travels. Space and time are events that could occur at the same time for one observer could occur at different times for another. This is a true time warp and occurs because of the planet's rotation.


Hubble telescope

The hubble telescope orbits outside distortion of the Earth's atmosphere. The hubble takes extremely high resolution images with negligible background light, allowing deep views of time and space. Many observations have lead to breakthroughs in astrophysics.
